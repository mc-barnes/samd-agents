### BLOCKERS

1. **Missing intended use population boundaries** — PRD states "neonatal patients" without specifying gestational age range, weight limits, or care setting (NICU vs. well-baby nursery). Per FDA's guidance on pulse oximetry (2013), intended use must define the specific patient population. Without boundaries, predicate comparison (510(k)) lacks specificity.
   - *Cite: 21 CFR 807.87(e); FDA Guidance "Pulse Oximeters — Premarket Notification Submissions" §IV.A*

2. **No software safety classification** — PRD does not assign an IEC 62304 safety class (A/B/C). Alarm management for SpO2 in neonates is patient-contacting safety-critical software. This classification drives the entire software development lifecycle process scope.
   - *Cite: IEC 62304:2006+A1:2015 §4.3*

### WARNINGS

1. **Alarm threshold rationale not traced to clinical evidence** — PRD specifies SpO2 alarm threshold of 88% but does not reference clinical literature supporting this value for the intended population. Reviewers will ask for the clinical basis.
   - *Cite: IEC 62366-1:2015+A1:2020 §5.1 (use specification); ISO 14971:2019 §5.5 (risk evaluation criteria)*

2. **Cybersecurity not addressed** — No mention of data at rest, data in transit, or SBOM requirements. Section 524B of the FD&C Act requires a cybersecurity plan for all cyber devices in premarket submissions.
   - *Cite: Section 524B, FD&C Act; FDA "Cybersecurity in Medical Devices" (2023) §IV*
